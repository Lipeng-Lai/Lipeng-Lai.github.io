As the night was fast falling, and as the moon, being past the full, would not rise early. We held a little council, a short one, for dearly our course was to lie by at the first lonely tavern we could find. So, they plied their oars once more, and I looked out for anything like a house. Thus we held on, speaking little, for four or five dull miles. It was very cold, and, a collier coming by us, with her gallery-fire smoking and faring, looked like a comfortable home.
